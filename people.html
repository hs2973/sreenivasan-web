postdoctoral fellows

  • Kübra Kömek Kırlı

    kk100@nyu.edu

    Kubra got her PhD from Carnegie Mellon University in Computational Neuroscience in 2014. Her dissertation focused on computational models of brain rhythm disturbances in schizophrenia. Before that, she completed her B.A. degrees in Mathematics and Computational Neuroscience at Skidmore College in 2010. She joined New York University Abu Dhabi in 2014 as a Post-Doctoral Associate and her current projects implement neuroimaging methodologies such as EEG and MEG combined with computational modelling approaches to gain better insight into how working memory works.

research staff

  • Israr Ul Haq

    israr@nyu.edu

    Israr did his M.B.B.S at the Aga Khan University Medical School in Karachi. During his studies he spent time as a visiting scholar at Duke University and Boston University to get more exposure to Neuroscience research. He continued on the research path after graduation by joining the Person Perception lab at NYU Abu Dhabi and then became a member of the Sreenivasan lab with burgeoning interests in Cognition and working memory. Israr aspires to specialize in Clinical Neurology while simultaneously pursuing his research interests in order to work both as a physician and a researcher in the future.
